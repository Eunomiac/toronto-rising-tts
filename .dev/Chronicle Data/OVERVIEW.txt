# Overview of TORONTO RISING

**Toronto Rising** is the current Chronicle of *Vampire: the Masquerade 5th Edition* that I am running. It is a sequel to **Toronto By Night**, a long-running Chronicle I ran five years ago in the same setting.

Unlike **Toronto By Night**, which told a "conventional" street-level story featuring a coterie of Anarch neonates, **Toronto Rising** tells a very different story. Based on the new ancilla-level character creation rules in the recently-released *In Memoriam* supplement, the PCs comprise the Camarilla prince and his court: some of the most powerful vampires in the city.

## Chronicle Introduction

The following was given to each player as an overview of the Chronicle I was inviting them to. It references many of the events played out during **Toronto By Night** (including the demise of the primary antagonist, the Tremere Frederik Scheer; the ascension of one of the PCs, the Ventrue Locke Ulrich, to Prince of Toronto; the appointment of another PC, Bacchus Giovanni, to represent the Hecata; and the rise of two more PCs—the Malkavian Johannes Napier and the Tremere Arthur Roy—to become co-Barons of the Anarchs):

> *Twelve years ago*, the Iron Prince of Toronto, Maximillian Steele of Clan Ventrue, abandoned his seat to **the Beckoning** after two centuries of strong, effective rule. A weak-willed ancillae, **Osborne Lowell**, rose to the throne as a figurehead controlled by **the Peerage** — a small council of primogen who went into hiding as the Sabbat threatened the city. Osborne Lowell’s rule was short, his **undignified removal** orchestrated by the very Peerage that created him. When the **tyrant-prince Scheer** fell to the **Good Doctors’** viral contagion, the cabal of primogen raised up former sheriff **Locke Ulrich**, whose star was on the rise thanks to his **successful infiltration of the local Anarch Movement**.
>
> Though never to eclipse the reign of the Iron Prince himself, **Prince Ulrich held Praxis for five tumultuous years.**
>
> **The Beckoning** continued to ravage Toronto. **Only two elders** are known to remain: **Bertrice**, Autarkis of Clan Gangrel, the disgraced primogen who now claims the northern borderlands of the city as her domain, and **the Island-Devil**, if rumors are to be believed. Autarkis of Clan Nosferatu, it is claimed that the creature terrorizes Centre Island, where it holds domain. **There are no others.** Agnes Bellanger, Drake, the Piece-Taker: **all of them Beckoned** by the Blood Gods to fight in their Gehenna War, leaving **their holdings abandoned** and the threads of **their many schemes undone**.
>
> **The Hecata**, the Sect of Death, still clutches Wychwood in a miasma of despair. Though known to only a few, the Hecata alone are immune to the Beckoning: **Bacchus Giovanni** remains their face, but powerful elders of the Samedi, the Cappadocians, and the Lamiae undoubtedly stalk the ossuaries beneath the Giovanni Estate.
>
> **The Ministry**, the Clan of the Snake, continues to offer salvation at **Redemption House**, where Ren treats excess with excess. And they have spread, with each of Ren’s three childer opening **Houses of their own**.
>
> **The Lasombra**, once the greatest enemy of the Camarilla, and **the Banu Haqim**, Assamites freed from the Tremere blood curse, **both now sit in membership** as proud pillars of the Ivory Tower.
>
> **The Anarchs**, under the mysterious new leadership of **the Good Doctors**, have spread like algal bloom.
>
> **Clan Tremere** pulls inward, fortifying their University Chantry, veiling themselves in insular secrecy.
>
> **The Nosferatu** confess to encounters with their Sabbat kin in dark places beneath the streets.
>
> And through it all, **Prince Ulrich’s leadership has been steady.**
>
> That is until **two months ago**, when Locke Ulrich was **staked before the Sun** by persons unknown.
>
> A struggle for power followed, during which a **coterie of ancillae** rose above their peers. They set their competitors against each other and climbed the chaos like a ladder: to power, to Praxis, **to the Domain of Toronto.**
>
> ## **That coterie is you.**
>
> **One of you is Prince of Toronto;** the others, your primogen, your officials, your counsel.
>
> **All of this is known.**
>
> What isn’t known is that, two weeks ago, **another one of you lured Prince Ulrich onto a rooftop and staked him before the Sun**.

## Key Lore & Setting Changes

I have made some changes to the official lore of *Vampire: the Masquerade*:

### The Hecata is a Sect, Not a Clan

In ***Toronto Rising,*** the Hecata is not a clan but a *sect* on par with the Camarilla or the Ashirra.  As such, the Hecata comprises multiple clans, several bloodlines nearly old enough to qualify as clans, and even the occasional member of a clan typically associated with a different sect entirely.

"Clan" Hecata has been split into two clans, Clan Cappadocian and Clan Giovanni. They share a history with many similarities to that of clans Tremere and Salubri: the original ancient progenitor clan, and the more-recent clan whose status was won after an act of diablerie. This increases the number of recognized clans by one, to fifteen (including the Salubri). Bloodlines originally restricted to Hecata characters have been split between Clan Cappadocian and Clan Giovanni to align with the lore.

#### Clan Cappadocian

Clan Cappadocian retains the official clan disciplines of the Hecata: Auspex, Fortitude, and Oblivion. Their Bane, however, has been changed:
> "Your singular affinity for death isolates you from your lost mortality far more dramatically than other Kindred.  Subtract your Bane Severity from your Humanity when determining the effects of low Humanity on yourself and your interactions with mortals."

Clan Cappadocian's bloodlines include the older lineages that, in previous editions, were given full clan treatments. The clan disciplines and clan banes of these bloodlines have been restored to their classic versions:

##### The Harbingers of Ashur

* **Disciplines:** Auspex, Fortitude, Oblivion
* **Bane:** "Your time in the Shadowlands (or, more likely, your tutelage under a sire who was among those who escaped across the Shroud) has bound your identity and sense of self into masks you feel compelled to wear. Whenever you are not wearing one of your masks, you suffer penalties to all Resolve and Composure rolls equal to your Bane Severity."

##### The Lamiae

* **Disciplines:** Auspex, Oblivion, Potence
* **Bane:** "The fangs of the Lamiae spread disease. A mortal victim must succeed at a Stamina roll against a difficulty equal to your Bane Severity. Upon failure, the mortal is afflicted with a disease chosen by the Storyteller. On a total failure, this disease is terminal within one week."

##### The Nagaraja

* **Disciplines:** Auspex, Dominate, Oblivion
* **Bane:** "The storied hunger of the Nagaraja cannot be denied for long. You can consume raw flesh and slake Hunger from it as if it were blood, but you subtract your Bane Severity from the amount of Hunger slaked by drinking blood alone."

##### The Samedi

* **Disciplines:** Fortitude, Oblivion, Obfuscate
* **Bane:** "Your return to unlife came with little regard for preserving your body against the ravages of the grave. Your skin has a ghastly pallor, and your flesh continues to rot, if slowly. You count as having the Repulsive Flaw (-2) and cannot increase your rating in the Looks Merit. Moreover, your appearance risks breaching the Masquerade if not concealed in some fashion. Any attempt to disguise yourself as human incurs a penalty to your dice pool equal to your Bane Severity."

#### Clan Giovanni

Clan Giovanni has had their classic discipline spread restored: Dominate, Oblivion, and Potence. Their Bane has not changed, though, since the Hecata Clan Bane already matches the classic bane of Clan Giovanni.

Bloodlines representing satellite families of the Giovanni (e.g. the Rossellinis, the Pisanob, the Milliners) have been assigned to this clan. Unlike the Cappadocian bloodlines, no rules changes were necessary as the Giovanni bloodlines classically shared their clan's disciplines and bane.

### Classic Clan Banes Have Been Restored

The restoration of classic clan banes does not stop with the Cappadocians and Giovanni: Wherever a bane significantly differed from its classical form, I've endeavored to change it back (while maintaining the mechanical connection to V5's "Bane Severity" trait).

* Clan Toreador now risks being mesmerized by great works of art
* Clan Nosferatu, like the Samedi, now have an appearance so monstrous it risks breaching the Masquerade if not disguised
* Clan Malkavian now suffers from a permanent Derangement
* the Banu Haqim, in addition to their V5 Bane, also suffer from the Curse of Growing Darkness (which darkens their skin as they age), and a third Bane depending on their caste: the Viziers suffer from obsession, and the Sorcerers suffer from a magical aura that is obvious to supernatural senses

The other clan banes described in V5 have been kept. Though some differ from their classic versions (e.g. Clan Tremere, Clan Ravnos), these differences are accurate reflections of recent developments in the V5 metaplot.

### Resonance Flavors Have Been Expanded from Four to Seven

In addition to the official resonance flavors (choleric, sanguine, melancholic and phlegmatic), I've added three more flavors, two of which simply involve giving names to flavors that are referenced only by description in the V5 official rules:

* **"Primal" Resonance:** The name given to "animal blood", resonating with Animalism and Protean. Per V5 rules, animal blood always contains fleetingly primal resonance—but now, the blood of particularly animalistic humans as well as werewolves can have primal resonance of any intensity.
* **"Ischemic" Resonance:** The name given to "blood empty of resonance", resonating with Oblivion.
* **"Mercurial" Resonance:** An entirely new flavor, whose emotions are "paradoxical, fluid, fatalistic, alien, inconsistent". It does not resonate with any discipline directly, though it is an important component of many thin-blood alchemy formulae and even the occasional Blood Sorcery ritual.

The three new flavors are considered "rare" compared to the other four, and generally need to be specifically sought out (thereby leaving the roll tables determining resonance flavor during a standard hunting test unchanged).

# **The Chronicle of Ash and Praxis: A History of Kindred Toronto**

## **On the Wilderness Before the City**

Long before there was a fort, before there was even a name worth preserving, the shores of Lake Ontario were already claimed.

The kine remember forests, trade routes, and the shifting territories of First Nations. The Damned remember something else: a hunting ground watched over by ancient eyes, a place where predators learned patience before they learned politics. Among those watchers was Drake of Clan Nosferatu, already old when Europe still mistook maps for truths.

Accounts differ as to when Drake crossed the Atlantic, but the most reliable fragments suggest he arrived in the New World alongside his childe, Fómorach, decades before any imperial charter would sanctify the land. They wandered the coast, then the rivers, then the shallow settlements that rose and fell with trade winds and winter starvation. Where other Kindred sought courts, Drake sought *infrastructure*: tunnels, caves, forgotten cellars, and later, the bones of roads not yet paved.

By the time the British Crown turned its gaze northward, Drake already knew every shadow worth inhabiting.

## **The Founding of Fort York and the First Nights**

When Fort York was established in 1793, the kine believed they were founding a garrison. The Damned recognized it as an invitation.

Simcoe's fort was not impressive by mortal standards, but it promised three things that Kindred had learned to value above all else: permanence, population, and plausible deniability. Where soldiers marched, civilians followed. Where civilians gathered, feeding became predictable. Where institutions formed, power could be hidden inside them.

It was during this period that the Camarilla formalized its claim over the region. Together, Drake and his childe mapped the underbelly of a city that barely existed yet, charting wells, basements, and forgotten storm channels like prophets cataloguing future veins. Toronto became a northern bulwark against the encroaching Sabbat domains of Windsor, Detroit, Hamilton, Ottawa, and above all Montreal. Where the Sword of Caine built cathedrals of violence, the Ivory Tower built courts of endurance.

The first Princes of Fort York are largely lost to time. Their reigns were short, their names consumed by wars that kine misremember as border conflicts and trade disputes. What remains is the shape of the institution they left behind: a city defined not by conquest, but by *holding the line*.

## **The Iron Praxis**

All meaningful political history in Toronto begins with the rise of the Iron Prince, Maximillian Steele of Clan Ventrue.

Steele assumed Praxis twenty-five years after Fort York's founding and ruled for the two centuries that followed. Even now, when elders vanish nightly into the Beckoning's maw, his reign stands as an aberration: not merely long, but *stable*. He was not simply Prince; he was the institutional memory of the city itself.

Steele's genius lay not in cruelty, nor even in charisma, though he possessed both. It lay in continuity. He transformed the Council of Primogen from a shifting alliance into a permanent organ of governance. He codified Elysium, enforced prestation with meticulous cruelty, and divided the city into domains that would remain largely intact for generations.

Under Steele, Toronto became a fortress without walls. Sabbat assaults shattered themselves against a court that refused to panic. Entire neighborhoods burned during conflicts kine recorded as riots, industrial accidents, or economic collapses. Steele's response was always the same: surgical violence, absolute secrecy, and an unwavering devotion to the Masquerade.

Around him gathered the figures who would define Toronto's long night. Agnes Bellanger returned to Casa Loma and transformed it into the cultural heart of undead society. Adrian Gerrard of the Brujah managed the financial arteries that fed the city. And through it all remained Drake who, it is said, secured P.A.T.H. for his clan by whispering three words into the Iron Prince's ear. No one knows what those words were. No one has ever asked.

And always, in Steele's shadow, stood his Seneschal: Frederik Scheer of the Tremere.

Scheer was Steele's instrument. Where Steele legitimized, Scheer enforced. The University Chantry rose under their joint patronage, embedding Tremere sorcery into hospitals, museums, and academic institutions until blood magic became indistinguishable from civic infrastructure.

## **The Red Industrial Century**

If Steele's early reign was about consolidation, his middle centuries were about endurance.

The nineteenth century transformed Toronto from colonial outpost into industrial city, and with it came the first true Sabbat wars. Windsor, Detroit, Hamilton, Ottawa, and Montreal ringed the city like a crown of thorns, each a Sword of Caine stronghold that viewed Toronto as both provocation and prize. Rail lines became invasion routes. Shipping lanes became hunting grounds. Factories became mass graves disguised as progress.

These wars were not singular events, but a condition of existence. Sabbat crusades erupted every few decades, each convinced it would finally break the Ivory Tower's northern fortress. Entire neighborhoods burned and were rebuilt under new names. Kine remembered labor riots, unexplained fires, and mysterious epidemics; Kindred remembered sieges, mass Embraces, and rivers running red through sewer tunnels Drake had mapped generations earlier.

Steele never pursued annihilation. He pursued attrition.

His strategy was simple and horrifyingly effective: never overextend, never escalate, and never let the city panic. Sabbat packs were bled dry, starved of recruits, and forced into wars of exhaustion against a court that could retreat into infrastructure and wait decades for vengeance. Steele allowed them to win battles so long as they lost the war.

It was during this era that Toronto earned its reputation among Kindred as *the city that refuses to die*.

## **The Quiet Dominion**

By the early twentieth century, the Sabbat had learned to fear Toronto.

Not because it was the strongest Camarilla domain, but because it was the most *adaptable*. The Iron Prince's court perfected a style of governance that avoided spectacle entirely. No grand rituals. No public executions. No proclamations worth recording. Power moved through zoning laws, hospital boards, universities, and banks. The Sabbat had few weaknesses to target, for the mechanisms of Camarilla control were masterfully obscured.

This was the age of Scheer's true ascendance. As Seneschal, he transformed the University Chantry into a machine of civic integration. Tremere wards were woven into urban planning. Sorcery became indistinguishable from bureaucracy. A hospital could refuse a patient for mortal reasons and occult ones simultaneously.

Drake expanded the undercity into something approaching a second Toronto, an invisible lattice of tunnels, bolt-holes, and dead-end corridors. Fómorach learned to treat the city as a living organism: arteries, nerves, and scars. Where kine saw growth, Nosferatu saw defensive architecture.

The Sabbat still attacked, but less frequently and with less conviction. They found no center to strike, no heart to rip out. Toronto did not resist them. It simply absorbed them and continued functioning.

This was the age in which the Iron Prince stopped being a just a ruler, and became a constant.

## **The Last War That No One Won**

The final decades of the Iron Praxis were the bloodiest in Toronto's history.

The Sabbat, sensing something shifting in the world, launched their most aggressive campaigns in over a century. Montreal sent wave after wave of crusaders. Detroit and Windsor fed them bodies. Hamilton became a staging ground for mass Embraces designed to overwhelm the Masquerade by sheer volume.

These were not wars of territory, but wars of ideology. The Sabbat believed the end was coming. They wanted Toronto to burn as proof that the old order could still be destroyed.

They failed.

But so did the Iron Prince.

The Camarilla held the city, but at enormous cost. Entire coteries were wiped out. Veterans emerged scarred, paranoid, and increasingly disconnected from mortal reality. The court survived, but it grew brittle. Too many Kindred learned to define themselves by siege conditions that no longer made sense in a world drifting toward digital surveillance and global conspiracy.

When the Sabbat finally withdrew across the Atlantic to fight their Gehenna War, Toronto did not celebrate.

It simply stood alone, exhausted, and quietly unsure what to do without an enemy.

It was only after this final war, when the city had learned how to survive everything except peace, that it faced a new threat—one that could not be resisted by force of arms or clever statesmanship: the Beckoning.

## **The Beckoning and the Long Silence**

The first true fracture in Toronto's history did not come from war, but from absence.

In the summer of 2017, Maximillian Steele answered the Beckoning and abandoned his throne. He departed for the Middle East, drawn by the same ancient summons that would soon claim elders across the world. For the first time in two centuries, Toronto stood without its Iron Prince.

What followed was not immediate collapse, but something more corrosive: uncertainty.

Elders vanished one by one. Alistair Etrata was Beckoned. Adrian Gerrard followed. Agnes Bellanger, Drake, and countless others either succumbed to the call or withdrew from public existence entirely. Entire domains were left abandoned. Long-brewing conspiracies froze mid-execution, their architects gone without explanation.

Only two elders are known to remain: Bertrice of the Gangrel, who withdrew north to Deer Park and Humewood, and the Island Devil, the Nosferatu autarkis who claims Centre Island and kills trespassers without parley.

All others are whispers, rumors, and sealed archives.

## **The Interregnum of Osborne Lowell**

Into this vacuum stepped Osborne Lowell.

Lowell was an ancilla elevated by the Peerage, a cabal of Primogen who believed that a weak Prince was preferable to an empty throne. His reign was brief, hollow, and humiliating.

Lowell ruled by permission, not authority. His decrees were ignored. His court was mocked. Sabbat pressure intensified as the Camarilla hesitated to act decisively. The Peerage, fearing exposure, eventually orchestrated his removal with the same quiet efficiency that had raised him.

Lowell's fall taught Toronto a brutal lesson: legitimacy could not be manufactured.

## **The Tyranny of Frederik Scheer**

Many speak of Scheer "seizing" Praxis, but this embellishes the facts. Scheer did not seize anything. He inherited power from a vacuum.

In 2019, Frederik Scheer claimed the throne and ruled openly as Prince. Where the Iron Prince had governed through endurance, Scheer ruled through terror. His loyalty to the Ivory Tower was absolute, but his interpretation of loyalty required obedience, surveillance, and ritualized cruelty.

Scheer weaponized the University Chantry. Blood sorcery became a tool of governance. Dissidents vanished. Court politics transformed into a labyrinth of favors enforced by thaumaturgical reprisal.

His reign lasted less than a year.

The Good Doctors of the Anarch Movement assassinated Scheer publicly using a bioweapon, killing him in full view of Camarilla and Anarch alike. The city breathed a collective sigh of relief that crossed every sect boundary.

## **Locke Ulrich and the Lie of Infiltration**

The Camarilla tells a comforting story about Locke Ulrich. They claim he was a loyal infiltrator, a secret agent planted among the Anarchs to undermine them from within. This is a lie, preserved to protect the dignity of those who elevated him.

Ulrich's defection was real. He left the Camarilla, joined the Anarchs, and helped build their movement. His return only came when the Peerage, desperate for stability, offered him Praxis. As childe of Osborne Lowell and a former sheriff in good standing before his defection, the Peerage reasoned—correctly, as it turned out—that history could be rewritten.

Ulrich ruled for five years. His reign was turbulent, but effective. He negotiated with Anarchs rather than exterminating them. He tolerated the Hecata. He restrained the Tremere. Under him, Toronto survived the worst years of the Beckoning without collapsing into open war.

And two weeks ago, he was staked before the sun.

## **These Fractured Nights**

The modern city is a mosaic of power vacuums.

The Hecata hold Wychwood, guarded by elders who do not answer the Beckoning. The Ministry spreads Redemption Houses like quiet infections. The Anarchs, under the shadow of the Good Doctors, proliferate through neighborhoods the Camarilla abandoned. Thin-blood alchemists haunt Kensington Market. Sabbat remnants lurk in the Distillery District.

And the elders are gone.

## **The Night of the Stake and the Ascension of Lucien St. Clair**

Ulrich's assassination shattered the last illusion of stability.

In the chaos that followed, a coterie of ancillae maneuvered with ruthless efficiency. They played rivals against each other, exploited old debts, and climbed the vacuum left by elder absence like a ladder.

At their head stands Lord Lucien St. Clair of Clan Toreador, descendant of the Count of St. Germain, now Prince of Toronto.

Around him gather Rashid Abdulrahman, Seneschal of the Banu Haqim; Aishe Tache, Malkavian Primogen; Fómorach, Nosferatu Primogen; Mylene Hamelin, Ventrue Primogen; and Black Caesar, Tremere Sheriff.

Together, they form a court in a city that has forgotten how to be ruled.

Drake is gone. Steele is gone. Scheer is dust. Ulrich burns beneath the sun.

What remains is a throne, a memory of legitimacy, and a city that has learned, too late, that immortality does not guarantee continuity.

And yet, Toronto endures, as it always has.  For the moment.

## The Free Chantry

The Free Chantry might look unimpressive, even harmless. After all, they're just four quiet academics who keep to themselves, preferring libraries to Elysiums. Do not believe this. These four Blood Sorcerers operate outside the University Chantry's authority, unfettered by restraints wisely imposed after centuries of hard lessons learned. They trade secrets across sect lines without asking questions, and they experiment without the institutional caution that serves as a leash on unchecked power. The Free Chantry sees freedom from oversight and precedent as an unalienable right, which might sound like something admirable... if they weren't so hungry for the kind of power that precious few can answer. Under the harsh light of this truth, their meek insistence that they just want to be left alone becomes anything *but* "meek". How long before these quiet rebels become your loudest problem?

# Historical Characters in ***Toronto Rising***

These characters are no longer present in Toronto, but played important roles in either the prequel Chronicle **Toronto By Night**, or the history of the city as a whole.

**NOTE:** Many of the descriptions below were written during the previous Chronicle, when the character was still active in some form or another, and have not been updated. Interpret each description with that in mind—if the header says the character is dead or has been Beckoned, that trumps anything the potentially-outdated description might say.

## Characters No Longer Active

### Maximillian Steele, (Former Prince) (Beckoned)
>
> "We will endure."

* Seventh Generation Elder of Clan Ventrue
* Status (Camarilla) 5
* Status (Clan Ventrue) 5

Toronto has been a Camarilla stronghold since its founding as Fort York over two hundred years ago, despite facing constant war with the surrounding Sabbat fortresses of Windsor, Detroit, Hamilton, Ottawa and, especially, Montreal. Prince Steele, Toronto's "Iron Prince" for most of that time, deserves much of the credit for the Camarilla's resilient hold over the city. Strong and fair, Prince Steele was a rarity among Kindred in more ways than one: He was respected and admired by almost all, and the time of his rule was one of stability and strength. The Camarilla lost an irreplaceable asset when, in the summer of 2017, the Iron Prince abandoned his domain for the Middle East. If the Sabbat hadn't also migrated en masse across the ocean, it is almost certain Toronto would have fallen in his absence.

### Frederik Scheer, (Former Prince, Former Regent of the University Chantry) (Dead)
>
> "The Camarilla has held Toronto for two hundred years, and we shall hold it for two hundred more."

* Eighth Generation Elder of Clan Tremere, House Tremere
* Status (Camarilla) 5
* Status (Clan Tremere) 5

Frederik Scheer has been a driving force behind Camarilla control over Toronto for at least two hundred years. As seneschal to the indomitable Prince Steele, Scheer developed a reputation for ruthless loyalty to the Ivory Tower. During Osborne Lowell's ineffectual reign, his power only grew, until he was able to seize Praxis for himself in 2019. He quickly became known as the Tyrant Prince due to his merciless, iron fisted rule, despite lasting not even a year. When the Good Doctors of the Anarchs (now co-Barons) killed Scheer in front of the entire Camarilla court with a bioweapon, Kindred across the city regardless of sect breathed a metaphorical sigh of relief.

### Alistair Etrata, (Former Banu Haqim Primogen) (Beckoned)
>
> "I am no Assamite. Those you call Assamites are the enemies of my people. Thralls to the blood god Ur-Shulgi, they cloister themselves at Alamut—for now."

* Sixth Generation Elder of the Banu Haqim, Vizier Caste
* Status (Camarilla) 5
* Status (the Banu Haqim) 4

Arriving in Toronto at the invitation of an Anarch is an unlikely start for a primogen. Nevertheless, he found himself in a city experiencing a power vacuum just as the Banu Haqim had been admitted into the Camarilla, and he exploited these circumstances adroitly—undoubtedly assisted by the strength of his old Blood. In a very short period of time, he demonstrated both his loyalty to the Camarilla and his ability to serve his clan as their primogen. His activities since have been subtle: either Alistair is wary of causing anyone to regret his appointment, or—more likely—he is guarding his intentions from everyone except, perhaps, his childe Sinclair.

The Oubliette. The Whisper-Weaver. The Vault.
