## Story (chronological summary)

### Origins and the New World

Fomórach grew up in a large Irish Catholic family with **more surviving siblings than the small holding could feed**. Scarcity shaped everyone: a brother was apprenticed as a carpenter after **three of five cows** were sold; **heirloom jewelry** went toward **dowries** for two sisters—by the time it was his turn, **little was left** to launch him. He chose **indentured servitude** hoping for **land in the New World**. He arrived **circa 1785** in a **mismanaged colony** he could see was failing even on the crossing; the **Puritan** character of the place chafed with his **Catholic** upbringing.

### Ghouling and the Blood

**Drake** took interest in him as a **source of information**. Fomórach excelled at **getting within earshot of important people** while going unnoticed—**resting “dumb” face**, **covert intelligence**, and unusual **resilience under the permanent hunger** of ghouldom (unlike colonists used to softer lives). After relocation **further north**, he **repeated** that performance under stress; Drake concluded he was **exceptional**. After Drake **narrowly survived a werewolf**, he valued **another pair of eyes** and the **fragility of the Wendigo line** as Drake’s **only childe**—and **embraced** Fomórach (**embraced at 17** on the timeline).

### Colonies and the Wendigo lesson

He **roved the colonies** with Drake learning what vampirism meant: **Puritan hunters in summer**, **hunger in winter**, **werewolves year-round**—a harsh education in being a **descendant of the Wendigo**.

### A Time of Violence (London)

**Released** by his sire, he sailed **back to Ireland** hoping to **find family** and offer the **gifts** he could grant—**eternal life**, **freedom from hunger**—at a **steep price**. Instead he found his **ancestral home ruined** under **British** depredations (famine-era horror). **Enraged**, he went to **London** and **cut a bloody path** through neighborhoods tied to the **Famine’s perpetrators**. He survived only through the **mercy of the London Nosferatu Primogen**—and was **returned to Toronto staked in a coffin**.

### A Time of Legacy (childer and bloodline)

After London, **Drake withheld trust** for **long decades**, driving home how much **favor and safety** Fomórach had **burned**; his revenge quest could have **tipped off** interested parties that the **Wendigo bloodline** was **not extinct**. Fomórach **studied** that legacy and acted to **reduce the line’s exposure**. In the **Ukraine**, he used the **Holodomor** as cover to seek someone with **fortitude** for the **beast of hunger** the blood demands. **Mara** took the blood **too well**: she **rejected Camarilla order** for a **personal crusade** that **continues**. A **second childe**, sired amid **Israel’s founding** and the **Palestinian Nakba**, **stayed loyal** though other choices remain **questionable**.

### A Time of Adversity (favor and the road)

The **Camarilla** runs on **tradition** and **favors**. A favor from long ago helped **Toronto** rise in the **1850s**; roughly **a century later** it was **called in**, passed **sire to childe**. **Oliver Gagnon** sought aid for his childe **Lucien**. Fomórach **retrieved Lucien from the Mojave**—the easy part—and the **drive back to Toronto** became an **“exciting five years,”** left deliberately vague on the board.

### A Time of Dominion (succession)

Facing **the Beckoning**, **Drake** confronted **impermanence**—not mortality, but **losing** a life’s work. He **trained Fomórach as successor**, no longer only **legbreaker** and **problem-solver** but **political operator**. Fomórach was drawn into **rule** earlier than most: **primogen council** work **from behind Drake’s veil** for **years** before Drake’s **Beckoning**.
