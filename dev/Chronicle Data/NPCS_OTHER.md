## The Wychwood Hecata

- the Hecata faction in the city, ostensibly led and represented by the ancilla Bacchus Giovanni, but with multiple elders and two methuselahs residing in the catacombs beneath the Giovanni Estate
- player-facing description should focus on the three known Giovanni members: Bacchus, the emissary; his aunt Lorenza, and her childe (Bacchus' cousin) Niccolo
- hint at the presence of the Baron's Bleak Bokor; he's important to our Tremere PC's backstory
- mention the fact that the Hecata are immune to the Beckoning, and as such, their elders have not been drawn away to the Gehenna War — making them a very powerful faction in the city
- also, recall that I've changed the setting somewhat: the Hecata is not a clan, but a sect comprising many clans, including Clan Giovanni and Clan Cappadocian.

### Player Introduction to the Wychwood Hecata

The Wychwood Hecata are Toronto's Sect of Death, and they possess Wychwood in the same way that a family possesses a burial plot: by insistent right and ritual. It is an ever-waning secret that, while the Camarilla's elders depart for the Beckoning, elder Hecata do not — the important takeaway being, the Hecata's old monsters stayed home. Bacchus Giovanni is the face you see at Elysium, honed and polished, smiling because he knows exactly the power he wields. His aunt Lorenza handles the work beneath the Estate, binding the dead to service with a calm and certain knowledge that does not require permission; his cousin, Niccolo, keeps the mortal money, the trusts, and the paper walls that make Wychwood viable and untouchable. Somewhere near the edge of all this, a pale creature whose history with Black Caesar spans centuries lurks... and that could be just the tip of the iceberg of horrors lurking under the dilapidated Giovanni Estate. How long before you're forced to decide between capitulation, and a conflict you cannot win?

**They represent your greatest rivals in Toronto: The skeletons in their closet are ancient, powerful, and they won't stop when you're dead.**

Niccolo Giovanni is old-school even by Giovanni standards. A financier of funerals and a broker of inherited guilt, he maintains the mortal shell companies and trust networks that keep Wychwood solvent and untouchable. He prefers contracts to confrontations, but his smile never reaches his eyes. Niccolo believes in hierarchy, in bloodlines, in consequence. If Bacchus is the diplomat, Niccolo is the accountant who decides what every alliance truly costs.
